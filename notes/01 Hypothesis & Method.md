# Hypothesis & Method

> [!important] Hypothesis
> **Tourism in Rotorua is (socially and environmentally) sustainable.**

Sub-questions:

1. **Environmental** — does tourism protect (or degrade) Rotorua's natural environment?
2. **Social** — does tourism benefit locals, respect Māori culture, and stay accessible and affordable?

We collect three kinds of evidence with the AIO-Survey app, then weigh them all in [[Conclusion]].

## 1 · Visitor & local survey

12 Likert questions (**5 = strongly agree … 1 = strongly disagree**, so higher = more sustainable), asked at each attraction. Results: [[Survey Stats]].

**Environmental** (q1, q2, q3, q4, q6, q9):

- q1 — This attraction manages rubbish effectively
- q2 — Recycling facilities are easy to access at this attraction
- q3 — The natural environment at this attraction is well looked after
- q4 — This attraction gives visitors clear information on how to reduce their environmental impact
- q6 — The attraction is not overcrowded
- q9 — From what you have seen, tourism in Rotorua protects the natural environment

**Social** (q5, q7, q8, q10, q11, q12):

- q5 — The attraction is accessible for a wide range of visitors, including families, elderly people and people with disabilities
- q7 — Public transport is easily accessible to get to and from this attraction
- q8 — The pricing for this attraction is reasonable
- q10 — Rotorua tourism represents Māori culture in a respectful way
- q11 — Tourism in Rotorua benefits the local community
- q12 — Rotorua is managing tourism in a way that can continue in the long run

## 2 · Environmental Quality Assessment (EQA)

At each site we score **7 categories × 3 features**. Each feature is a penalty: 0 (fine), 2 (minor issue), 4 (major issue). Final score = **84 − total deductions** — higher is better. Results: [[EQA Scores]].

| Category | Looks at |
| --- | --- |
| Waste & recycling | litter, finding bins, bins being used |
| Natural landscape & biodiversity | ecosystem health, native species, track condition |
| Pollution & resource management | cleanliness, noise, resource use |
| Visitor pressure & crowding | space, crowd management, barriers |
| Accessibility & visitor facilities | disability access, facilities, transport |
| Cultural sustainability | te reo, respect, iwi involvement |
| Sustainability education & management | info, encouragement, programmes |

## 3 · Interviews

Recorded with the app (or uploaded from Voice Memos), transcribed with Deepgram, then Gemini summarises each one and classifies every point the speaker made:

- **stance** — supports / contradicts / neutral (toward the hypothesis)
- **dimension** — social / environmental / general

Every interview becomes a note in `Interviews/`, and every point lands on the [[Evidence Board]].

> [!warning] AI labels are a starting point, not a verdict
> Read the transcript before quoting anyone in the final report. If you disagree with a classification, say so below the marker line in that interview's note.
